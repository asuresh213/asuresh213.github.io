Hi! I’m Arun, a math PhD student at University of Missouri. I am currently doing my doctoral studies with Dr. Dan Edidin. In my research, I try to utilize results from algebraic geometry and representation theory to approach problems in signal processing, and applied mathematics at large. The exact specifics of my research are listed here. $\\$
I am originally from Chennai, a coastal city in South India, and I moved to the U.S. in the fall of 2016. I got my dual B.S/M.S. in math from Georgia State University, where I focused on commutative algebra. During this time I worked with Dr. Florian Enescu and wrote a thing about numerical semigroup rings.

In addition to research and teaching:

  1. I run the math directed readings program (DRP).
  2. I cohost the weekly graduate student seminar.
  3. I serve as the president of the graduate student chapter of the AMS.

Feel free to send me an email (aszxy [AT] umsystem [DOT] edu) if you would like to get involved.

When I am not thinking about math, I am usually writing about math here. When I am not writing about math, I am usually biking through rural Missouri or climbing rocks.
